# Best Webinar Platforms in 2025: Host Engaging Online Events and Training

Webinars generate 20-40% of B2B leads for companies that use them consistently. The best webinar platforms combine professional streaming quality with engagement tools — polls, Q&A, chat, and handouts — and automate follow-up email sequences. Monthly search volume: ~30,000/month.

## Types of Webinars

- **Live webinars:** Real-time with audience interaction
- **Automated webinars:** Pre-recorded presented as live
- **Hybrid:** Live host + pre-recorded segments
- **On-demand:** Recorded and available anytime

---

## Top 7 Webinar Platforms for 2025

### 1. Zoom Webinars — Best for Large-Scale Events

**Best for:** Companies hosting large webinars (500-50,000 attendees) with enterprise reliability

Zoom Webinars extends Zoom Meetings into a broadcast format — attendees are view-only (can't accidentally unmute), Q&A is moderated, and panelists appear on stage. The most reliable option for large audiences.

**Strengths:**
- Supports up to 50,000 attendees
- Panelist/attendee distinction (no accidental interruptions)
- Polls, Q&A, hand raising, chat
- Record to cloud or local
- Registration page builder
- Integrates with HubSpot, Salesforce, Marketo

**Limitations:**
- Requires existing Zoom license + Webinar add-on
- Less engaging than purpose-built webinar tools
- No automated webinar functionality

**Pricing:** Webinar 500 $149/month; Webinar 1000 $340/month; Webinar 3000 $690/month (add-on to Zoom Pro)

---

### 2. Demio — Best for Marketing Webinars

**Best for:** Marketing teams running lead generation and product demonstration webinars

Demio is purpose-built for marketing webinars — beautiful registration pages, engagement tools, automated email sequences, and marketing integrations create a polished attendee experience that converts.

**Strengths:**
- Beautiful, branded registration pages
- Automated email sequences (before + after webinar)
- Engagement: polls, handouts, featured Q&A, CTAs
- Live, automated, and on-demand webinar modes
- HubSpot, Drip, ConvertKit integrations
- In-room CTAs that convert attendees during the webinar

**Limitations:**
- Max 1,000 attendees (more expensive for larger events)
- No breakout rooms
- Less suitable for training vs. marketing webinars

**Pricing:** Starter $59/month (50 attendees); Growth $109/month (150 attendees); Premium $220/month (500 attendees)

---

### 3. StreamYard — Best for Live Streaming and Webinars

**Best for:** Content creators and companies who want to multistream webinars to YouTube, LinkedIn, and Facebook simultaneously

StreamYard runs entirely in the browser — invite guests, add branded overlays, and stream live to YouTube, LinkedIn Live, Facebook, Twitch, and your own website simultaneously with one click.

**Strengths:**
- Multistream to 8 platforms simultaneously
- Browser-based (no software download)
- Branded overlays, banners, and backgrounds
- Guest links for remote panelists
- Stream to your website via embed
- Record locally for repurposing

**Limitations:**
- Not a traditional webinar tool (no registration pages)
- Attendee interaction via comment sections on platforms
- Less analytics than Demio or WebinarJam

**Pricing:** Free (limited, StreamYard watermark); Basic $49/month; Professional $99/month

---

### 4. WebinarJam — Best for Sales Webinars

**Best for:** Sales teams and course creators running high-conversion sales webinars

WebinarJam is optimized for sales — its Panic Button (instantly kick disruptive attendees), Always-On Room, and one-click upsells are designed for webinars where conversion is the primary goal.

**Strengths:**
- One-click upsell and product offers during webinar
- Automated replay room for follow-up
- Pop-up CTAs timed to key moments
- Attendee spotlight (bring audience member on screen)
- Injection technology (works despite firewalls)
- Integrates with 1,000+ tools via Zapier

**Limitations:**
- Interface less modern than Demio
- Occasional reliability issues reported
- Annual commitment required

**Pricing:** Basic $39/month; Professional $79/month; Enterprise $229/month (billed annually)

---

### 5. EverWebinar — Best for Automated Webinars

**Best for:** Course creators running "automated" or "evergreen" webinars 24/7

EverWebinar (sister product to WebinarJam) makes pre-recorded webinars appear live — with simulated attendee counts, chat replays, and scheduled broadcast times. Perfect for passive lead generation and sales at any hour.

**Strengths:**
- Automated webinars that feel live
- Simulated live chat and attendee activity
- Multiple scheduled show times
- Email follow-up sequences
- Integration with WebinarJam for live-to-auto repurposing
- Conversion tracking and analytics

**Limitations:**
- "Fake live" approach is controversial
- Annual commitment required
- No real-time audience interaction

**Pricing:** Basic $32/month; Professional $58/month; Enterprise $185/month (billed annually)

---

### 6. Riverside.fm — Best for High-Quality Webinar Recording

**Best for:** Podcasters, educators, and creators who need broadcast-quality recordings from remote guests

Riverside records each participant locally in uncompressed audio and 4K video, then syncs the files — eliminating internet quality degradation. The result is studio-quality content from remote participants worldwide.

**Strengths:**
- Local recording = studio quality regardless of internet
- Separate audio/video tracks per participant
- AI-powered transcription and clip creation
- Magic Clips for social media repurposing
- Screen sharing and presentation support

**Limitations:**
- Primarily a recording tool, not a webinar platform
- No registration pages or marketing features
- Not suitable for large audiences

**Pricing:** Free (2 hours/month); Standard $15/month; Pro $24/month

---

### 7. BigMarker — Best for Enterprise Webinar Programs

**Best for:** Enterprise teams running large-scale webinar programs with advanced customization

BigMarker's enterprise platform handles webinars, summits, hybrid events, and on-demand academies at scale. Its white-label capabilities, advanced analytics, and Salesforce integration make it the enterprise standard.

**Strengths:**
- White-label webinar environment
- Up to 10,000 attendees
- Multi-session virtual summits
- Advanced analytics and attribution
- CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot, Marketo)
- On-demand video content management

**Limitations:**
- Expensive for small teams
- Complexity requires onboarding
- Overkill for simple webinar needs

**Pricing:** Starter $99/month; Elite $299/month; Premier $399/month; White Label custom

## FAQ

### What is the best webinar platform for beginners?

Demio is the most beginner-friendly — its setup wizard, beautiful templates, and built-in marketing integrations require no technical expertise. Zoom Webinars is the easiest if you already use Zoom.

### What is an automated webinar?

An automated webinar is a pre-recorded presentation delivered on a schedule (daily, weekly, or on-demand) as if it were live. Platforms like EverWebinar and Demio simulate live elements (chat, polls) to maintain engagement.

### How many people can attend a webinar?

It depends on the platform: Demio supports up to 1,000, WebinarJam up to 5,000, BigMarker up to 10,000, and Zoom Webinars up to 50,000. Most small business webinars have 50-500 attendees.
