Microsoft Excel remains one of the most in-demand skills in virtually every industry. Whether you're in finance, marketing, operations, or data analysis, Excel proficiency can significantly boost your career prospects. With over 1.2 million monthly searches, "best Excel courses online" reflects how many professionals are looking to level up their spreadsheet skills in 2025.

This guide covers the top Excel courses available online — both free and paid — organized by skill level and use case.

## Why Excel Skills Still Matter in 2025

Despite the rise of Python, Power BI, and Tableau, Excel is still used by over 750 million people worldwide. It's the lingua franca of business data. Financial models, budget trackers, sales dashboards, HR reports — all of these live in Excel.

Advanced Excel users earn significantly more than basic users. According to PayScale, adding Excel to your skill set can increase your salary by 5–10%, and certain Excel-heavy roles like financial analyst or business intelligence analyst can command six-figure salaries.

Key Excel skills employers look for:
- Advanced formulas (VLOOKUP, INDEX/MATCH, XLOOKUP, IF, SUMIFS)
- Pivot Tables and Pivot Charts
- Power Query and data cleaning
- Data visualization and dashboards
- Macros and VBA automation
- Power BI integration

## Best Free Excel Courses

### 1. Microsoft Excel — Free Training (Microsoft)
Microsoft itself offers free training at support.microsoft.com/excel. The courses cover beginner through advanced topics with video lessons and practice files. Since it comes directly from Microsoft, the content is always up to date with the latest Excel features.

**Best for**: Learning specific features quickly, reference material

### 2. GCFGlobal Excel Tutorials
GCFGlobal.org has been providing free digital literacy courses for over 20 years. Their Excel tutorials cover everything from opening a spreadsheet to creating charts and using formulas. The content is beginner-friendly with interactive exercises.

**Best for**: Absolute beginners with no prior Excel experience

### 3. ExcelJet (Free Formulas & Tips)
While not a structured course, exceljet.net is the best free reference site for Excel formulas and functions. Every function has clear explanations, multiple examples, and common use cases. Bookmark this immediately.

**Best for**: Formula reference and troubleshooting

### 4. Chandoo.org
Purna Duggirala (Chandoo) has been teaching Excel for over 15 years. The site has hundreds of free tutorials, downloadable templates, and a very active community forum. Topics range from basic formatting to advanced dashboard design.

**Best for**: Intermediate users who learn well from community-driven content

### 5. YouTube: Leila Gharani
Leila Gharani's YouTube channel has over 2 million subscribers and covers Excel comprehensively — from basic formulas to Power Query, Power Pivot, and advanced dashboard design. Her teaching style is clear, visual, and practical.

**Best for**: Visual learners who prefer video over text

## Best Paid Excel Courses

### 1. Microsoft Excel — Excel from Beginner to Advanced (Udemy)
This is consistently one of the best-selling courses on Udemy, with over 900,000 students enrolled. Instructor Kyle Pew covers:
- Excel fundamentals and interface
- 25+ formulas with real examples
- Pivot Tables and charts
- Conditional formatting and data validation
- Macros and basic VBA

**Price**: $15–20 during sales (Udemy runs sales constantly)
**Duration**: 17+ hours
**Rating**: 4.7/5 from 400,000+ reviews
**Best for**: Complete beginners wanting a structured path from zero to advanced

### 2. Excel Skills for Business Specialization (Coursera — Macquarie University)
This four-course specialization from Macquarie University is taught by Dr. Prashan Karunaratne and is one of the highest-rated Excel courses anywhere online.

Courses include:
- Essentials
- Intermediate I
- Intermediate II
- Advanced

**Price**: $49/month (audit for free)
**Duration**: 6 months at 5 hours/week
**Certificate**: Yes, shareable on LinkedIn
**Best for**: Professionals who want a credentialed, structured curriculum

### 3. Everyday Excel (Coursera — University of Colorado)
A beginner-to-intermediate course focused on real business applications. Covers spreadsheet design, formulas, data manipulation, and visualization. Less depth than the Macquarie specialization but faster to complete.

**Price**: Audit free
**Best for**: Learners who want a university-backed certificate quickly

### 4. Excel for Finance Professionals (CFI — Corporate Finance Institute)
If your Excel use is finance-focused, CFI's courses are industry-standard. Their Financial Modeling and Valuation Analyst (FMVA) program is used by investment banks and consulting firms.

Topics include:
- Financial modeling in Excel
- DCF valuation
- LBO models
- Sensitivity analysis and scenario modeling

**Price**: $497/year (full program)
**Best for**: Finance analysts, investment bankers, consultants

### 5. Excel VBA and Macros (Udemy — Chris Dutton)
For users who want to automate repetitive tasks, this course covers:
- Recording and editing macros
- VBA fundamentals (variables, loops, conditions)
- UserForms and dialog boxes
- Automating common Excel workflows

**Price**: $15–20 on sale
**Duration**: 12 hours
**Best for**: Intermediate users who want to automate workflows

## What to Learn First: Recommended Learning Path

**Month 1 — Foundations**
- Interface navigation and formatting
- Basic formulas: SUM, AVERAGE, COUNT, IF
- Absolute vs. relative references
- VLOOKUP and XLOOKUP

**Month 2 — Intermediate**
- Nested IF statements
- INDEX/MATCH
- SUMIFS, COUNTIFS, AVERAGEIFS
- Pivot Tables basics
- Charts and visualization

**Month 3 — Advanced**
- Power Query for data cleaning
- Advanced Pivot Tables and slicers
- Data validation and conditional formatting
- Dashboard design

**Month 4+ — Specialization**
- Choose: Finance modeling, VBA automation, or Power BI

## Excel Certification Worth Getting

**Microsoft Office Specialist (MOS) Excel**
Microsoft's official certification validates your Excel skills to employers. There are three levels:
- Associate (Excel 2019 or Microsoft 365)
- Expert (advanced features)
- Master (all Office apps)

The exam costs around $100 and is widely recognized by employers. Study using Microsoft's free official guide, then practice with MOS exam prep books.

Whether you're starting from scratch or trying to move from intermediate to advanced, there's an Excel course at the right level for you.
