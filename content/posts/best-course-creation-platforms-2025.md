# Best Course Creation Platforms in 2025: Sell Online Courses and Build Your Academy

The global e-learning market is projected to reach $840 billion by 2030, and course creators are capturing a growing share of that revenue. The right platform handles hosting, payments, student management, and marketing — so you can focus on creating great content. Monthly search volume: ~35,000/month.

## What Separates Great Course Platforms

- **Content hosting:** Video, audio, PDFs, quizzes, live sessions
- **Student experience:** Progress tracking, certificates, community
- **Marketing tools:** Sales pages, email sequences, affiliate programs
- **Payment flexibility:** One-time, subscriptions, payment plans
- **Analytics:** Completion rates, revenue, student engagement

---

## Top 8 Course Creation Platforms for 2025

### 1. Teachable — Best for First-Time Course Creators

**Best for:** Coaches, consultants, and educators launching their first online course

Teachable has helped over 100,000 creators earn more than $1 billion in course sales. Its clean interface, guided setup process, and built-in sales page builder make launching a first course faster than any competitor.

**Strengths:**
- Guided course setup wizard
- No-code sales page builder
- One-click upsells and order bumps
- Built-in affiliate program
- Student Q&A and comment sections
- Certificate of completion generator

**Limitations:**
- Transaction fees on free and Basic plans
- Live sessions require Zoom integration
- Community features less developed than Kajabi

**Pricing:** Free (5% transaction fee); Basic $59/month; Pro $159/month; Business $665/month

---

### 2. Thinkific — Best for Scaling Course Businesses

**Best for:** Course creators who want powerful customization and no transaction fees from day one

Thinkific offers unlimited courses, students, and video hosting on its free plan with zero transaction fees — the most generous entry point in the market. Its App Store adds community, coaching, and marketing functionality.

**Strengths:**
- No transaction fees on any plan
- Unlimited courses and students on paid plans
- Powerful course builder with 10+ content types
- Communities (Thinkific Communities add-on)
- White-label branded learning environment
- Thinkific App Store for extensions

**Limitations:**
- Email marketing requires integration
- Live classes require separate tools
- Community features cost extra

**Pricing:** Free (1 course, limited); Basic $36/month; Start $74/month; Grow $149/month

---

### 3. Kajabi — Best All-in-One Creator Business Platform

**Best for:** Serious creators wanting courses, membership, email, website, and podcast in one

Kajabi is the premium all-in-one solution — it replaces Teachable (courses) + ConvertKit (email) + Circle (community) + your website builder. Higher cost, but potentially eliminates multiple subscriptions.

**Strengths:**
- All-in-one: courses + email + community + website + podcast
- Beautiful, modern design templates
- Pipelines (visual funnel builder)
- Built-in email marketing with automation
- No transaction fees
- 24/7 customer support

**Limitations:**
- Most expensive option ($149+ per month)
- Limited students on entry plans
- Community less advanced than Circle

**Pricing:** Kickstarter $69/month (25 customers); Basic $149/month; Growth $199/month; Pro $399/month

---

### 4. Podia — Best for Digital Product Bundles

**Best for:** Creators selling courses alongside digital downloads, memberships, and coaching

Podia lets you sell everything in one storefront — online courses, digital downloads, memberships, webinars, and coaching sessions. Its simplicity and zero transaction fees make it a strong Teachable alternative.

**Strengths:**
- Zero transaction fees on all plans
- Courses + downloads + memberships + coaching
- Built-in email marketing (up to 250 subscribers)
- Affiliate program
- Clean, modern student dashboard
- Unlimited everything on paid plans

**Limitations:**
- Design customization limited
- Analytics less detailed than Kajabi
- No mobile app for students

**Pricing:** Free (8% transaction fee); Mover $39/month; Shaker $89/month

### 5. LearnWorlds — Best for Interactive Course Experiences

**Best for:** Educators and training companies wanting the most interactive, engaging student experience

LearnWorlds specializes in interactive video learning — add clickable areas, quizzes within videos, branching scenarios, and SCORM content. Its white-label mobile app makes it perfect for professional training companies.

**Strengths:**
- Interactive video with in-video questions
- SCORM and xAPI support
- White-label mobile apps (iOS + Android)
- Social learning features (student collaboration)
- Advanced assessments and certificates
- B2B bulk course selling

**Limitations:**
- Complex for simple course use cases
- Higher price for advanced features
- Steeper learning curve than Teachable

**Pricing:** Starter $24/month; Pro Trainer $79/month; Learning Center $249/month; High Volume custom

### 6. Udemy (for Instructors) — Best for Marketplace Reach

**Best for:** Instructors who want access to Udemy's 57 million student marketplace

Creating a course on Udemy gives you immediate access to one of the world's largest learning audiences without building your own audience first. The trade-off: Udemy controls pricing and keeps 50-75% of revenue.

**Strengths:**
- 57+ million student marketplace
- Zero upfront cost to publish
- Udemy's SEO and discovery brings organic students
- Promotional emails to interested students
- Structured curriculum builder

**Limitations:**
- Udemy keeps 50-75% of revenue
- Limited pricing control
- Compete with thousands of similar courses
- Cannot build email list from Udemy students

**Pricing:** Free to create; Udemy keeps 50% on organic sales, 75% on promotional sales

### 7. Circle — Best Course + Community Combo

**Best for:** Educators who prioritize community learning alongside course content

Circle combines online courses with a thriving community platform — live streams, discussion spaces, member profiles, and events create a learning environment where students support each other.

**Strengths:**
- Excellent community + courses integration
- Live streams and events built-in
- Beautiful modern interface
- Gamification and member profiles
- Course progress tracking
- Custom domain and branding

**Limitations:**
- More expensive than Teachable
- No built-in email marketing
- Overkill if community isn't a priority

**Pricing:** Basic $89/month; Professional $199/month; Business $360/month

---

### 8. Gumroad — Best for Simple Digital Product + Course Selling

**Best for:** Creators testing their first course with minimal setup and no monthly fees

Gumroad requires zero upfront investment — list your course, share the link, and collect payments. Its simplicity is unmatched for validating a course idea before committing to a full platform.

**Strengths:**
- No monthly fees (revenue percentage model)
- Set up in under 30 minutes
- Sell courses, ebooks, and templates
- Email list integration
- Discover page for organic traffic

**Limitations:**
- 10% transaction fee (on free plan)
- Limited learning experience vs. Teachable
- No community or progress tracking

**Pricing:** Free (10% fee); Premium $10/month (0% fee)

## How to Choose the Right Course Platform

**Just starting out:** Thinkific free plan or Gumroad — test your idea before committing to monthly fees.

## FAQ

### Which course platform has the lowest fees?

Thinkific has zero transaction fees on all plans. Kajabi, Podia, and LearnWorlds also have no transaction fees on paid plans. Teachable charges 5% on its free plan and Gumroad charges 10%.

### Can you make money selling online courses?

Yes — the average course creator on Teachable earns $5,000-$50,000 per year, with top creators earning millions. Success depends on audience size, niche expertise, course quality, and marketing.

### How long does it take to create an online course?

A basic 4-6 module course takes 40-80 hours to create from scratch (planning, recording, editing, uploading). Most creators spend 2-4 weeks on their first course while working on it part-time.

### Is Kajabi worth the price?

For creators earning $3,000+/month in course revenue, Kajabi typically replaces 4-5 separate tools (email, website, community, course platform, podcast host), making the $149-$199/month price justified. Below that threshold, Teachable or Thinkific are more cost-effective.
