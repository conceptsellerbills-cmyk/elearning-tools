## Teaching Online Is a Business — Treat It Like One

Online tutoring has exploded since 2020. The global online tutoring market will exceed $200 billion by 2026. Individual tutors are building six-figure businesses teaching everything from SAT prep to music theory to SQL.

But teaching knowledge is only half the equation. Your tools determine whether students book again, whether sessions run smoothly, and whether you can scale beyond 20 hours per week. Here's the tech stack that separates professional online tutors from amateurs.

## Video Conferencing: The Foundation

Your video platform is where tutoring actually happens. Get this right first.

### Zoom — Best for Professional Tutoring

Zoom remains the most reliable, feature-rich video platform for tutoring. Features tutors specifically need:

- **Whiteboard**: Built-in whiteboard for math, diagrams, and concepts
- **Screen sharing**: Student can share their screen for you to review work
- **Annotation on screen share**: Draw on top of their work in real time
- **Breakout rooms**: Useful for group tutoring sessions
- **Recording**: Record sessions for students to review (students love this)
- **Waiting room**: Professional intake experience

**Pricing**: Basic free (40-minute limit). Pro: $14.99/month (unlimited meetings).

### Google Meet — Best Free Option

For tutors starting out, Google Meet works well:
- No time limit on sessions (for Google account holders)
- Screen sharing and chat
- Works in browser (no download required for students)
- Free with any Google account

**Limitation**: Less feature-rich than Zoom. No built-in whiteboard as good as Zoom's.

## Virtual Whiteboards

A good virtual whiteboard is non-negotiable for math, science, and any visual teaching.

### Miro — Best Collaborative Whiteboard

Miro is an infinite canvas that supports real-time collaboration. Both you and the student can draw, write, and arrange content simultaneously.

**Tutoring-specific uses:**
- Math problems worked step-by-step
- Concept maps and diagrams
- Essay outlining and annotation
- SAT/ACT practice problem review

**Pricing**: Free tier available (3 boards). Starter: $8/month for unlimited boards.

### Bitpaper — Built Specifically for Tutors

Bitpaper was designed for tutoring. It opens in a browser, requires no account for students, and has a clean mathematical notation focus.

**Features:**
- Infinite canvas
- PDF upload (import homework, textbook pages)
- Notation tools
- Instant share link (no student account required)

**Pricing**: Free for up to 2 papers/week. Pro: $12/month.

### GoodNotes / Notability (iPad)

If you have an iPad with Apple Pencil, GoodNotes or Notability + screen sharing creates the most natural handwriting experience — students can see natural handwritten math solutions.

## Scheduling and Booking

Manual scheduling via email is a time drain. Automate it.

### Calendly — Best for Simple Booking

Students book sessions directly from your availability. No back-and-forth emails.

**Setup:**
- Connect your calendar (Google Calendar or Outlook)
- Set your available hours
- Share your Calendly link in your email signature and website
- Students pick a time, automatically get a Zoom link

**Add buffer time**: Calendly automatically adds 10-minute buffers between sessions so you're never rushed.

**Pricing**: Free (basic). Standard: $10/month for more customization.

### Acuity Scheduling — Best for Multiple Services

Acuity is better than Calendly when you offer different session types (60-min SAT prep, 45-min homework help, 90-min intensive) at different prices.

**Features:**
- Multiple service types and durations
- Package booking (buy 10 sessions, schedule over time)
- Stripe payment integration
- Intake questionnaire (ask what they want to cover before the session)
- Automated reminder emails

**Pricing**: $16/month.

## Payment Processing

Don't chase invoices. Collect payment before or at booking.

### Stripe — Best for Direct Payment

Integrate Stripe with your booking system. Students enter credit card at booking. You get paid automatically.

Works with Acuity, Calendly, and most scheduling tools.

**Fees**: 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. No monthly fee.

### Venmo/PayPal — For Informal Arrangements

Works fine for individual arrangements but creates reconciliation headaches at scale. Use Stripe for a professional setup.

## Communication and Resources

### Loom — Async Video Explanations

Record short video explanations between sessions. "I reviewed your essay — here's my feedback" delivered as a 5-minute video is more valuable than a written comment.

Students can rewatch key explanations as many times as needed. This adds perceived value without adding your time.

**Pricing**: Free (25 videos). Business: $12.50/month.

### Notion — Session Notes and Resources

Create a Notion page for each student. Share notes, resources, and homework assignments. Students can comment and ask questions between sessions.

This creates a professional client portal experience without expensive software.

## The Complete Tutor Tech Stack

**Starting out (under $30/month total):**
- Video: Zoom Pro ($15/month)
- Whiteboard: Bitpaper free or Miro free
- Scheduling: Calendly free
- Payment: Stripe (transaction fees only)

**Scaling (30-50 sessions/week):**
- Video: Zoom Pro ($15/month)
- Whiteboard: Miro Starter ($8/month)
- Scheduling + Payments: Acuity ($16/month) + Stripe
- Async video: Loom Business ($12.50/month)
- Student notes: Notion (free)

**Total: ~$51.50/month** to run a professional operation capable of handling 40+ weekly sessions.

At even $40/hour and 20 hours/week, that's $800/week gross. The tools pay for themselves on the first session of the month.
