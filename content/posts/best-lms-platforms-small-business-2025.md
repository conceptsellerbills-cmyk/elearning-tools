## Why Small Businesses Need an LMS

Employee turnover costs businesses 50-200% of the departed employee's annual salary. A significant driver is lack of development opportunities — employees leave when they stop growing.

A Learning Management System (LMS) centralizes training, onboarding, compliance, and skill development in one place. Small businesses use LMS platforms to:

- Onboard new employees 40% faster
- Ensure compliance training completion (with completion tracking and certificates)
- Reduce training costs (record once, train forever)
- Scale training as the team grows without proportional cost increases

Here are the best LMS platforms sized for small and mid-sized businesses.

## TalentLMS — Best Overall for Small Business

TalentLMS is the most widely used LMS for small businesses because it balances features with usability. Setup takes hours, not weeks.

**Key features:**
- Unlimited courses (paid plans)
- SCORM and xAPI compatible (import existing training content)
- Branch portals (separate portals for different departments or clients)
- Gamification: points, badges, leaderboards
- Automated completion certificates
- Mobile app for iOS and Android
- Salesforce, Zendesk, and BambooHR integrations

**What makes it small-business friendly:**
- No IT department required for setup
- Import PowerPoints, videos, and PDFs directly
- Free tier for up to 5 users (genuinely useful for getting started)

**Pricing**: Free (5 users, 10 courses). Core: $89/month (40 users). Growth: $179/month (unlimited users, up to 500).

## Teachable — Best for Selling Courses Externally

If you want to train your own team AND sell courses to customers or clients, Teachable handles both in one platform.

**Key features:**
- Build beautiful course landing pages
- Video hosting included
- Quizzes, completion certificates
- Affiliate program for external course sales
- Student community features
- Upsells and course bundles

**Limitations**: Less suited for compliance training or complex HR workflows. More course-creator tool than traditional LMS.

**Pricing**: Free (limited). Basic: $39/month. Pro: $119/month.

**Best for**: Coaches, consultants, and businesses that want to monetize their training content.

## Thinkific — Best for Course Quality

Similar to Teachable but with stronger course-building tools and more design flexibility.

**Standout features:**
- Drag-and-drop course builder
- Community features built in (discussion forums, live lessons)
- Stripe and PayPal integration
- Strong mobile experience
- App store with integrations

**Pricing**: Free (1 course, 1 community). Basic: $36/month. Start: $74/month. Grow: $149/month.

**Best for**: Businesses focused on high-quality learning experiences.

## Docebo — Best for Growing Companies

Docebo is a more sophisticated LMS designed to scale with your business. AI-powered content recommendations and social learning features set it apart.

**Key features:**
- AI-powered personalized learning paths
- Social learning and peer-to-peer knowledge sharing
- Extended enterprise (train partners and customers, not just employees)
- Formal certification management
- Deep HR system integrations (Workday, SAP, BambooHR)

**Pricing**: Custom (typically $25,000+/year). Best for companies with 200+ employees.

**Best for**: Companies planning significant growth who want an enterprise-grade platform.

## Absorb LMS — Best for Compliance Training

Absorb specializes in compliance-heavy industries: healthcare, financial services, manufacturing. Tracking, reporting, and audit trails are first-class.

**Key features:**
- Automated compliance deadline tracking
- Mandatory course enrollment
- Comprehensive audit reports
- eCommerce for external training sales
- Mobile-first design

**Pricing**: Custom. Mid-range pricing typically $14,000-$20,000/year.

## Google Classroom — Best Free Option

If you're a small team on a tight budget, Google Classroom is completely free and integrates seamlessly with Google Workspace.

**Capabilities:**
- Create and distribute assignments
- Video lessons with Google Meet
- Quizzes via Google Forms
- Grade tracking

**Limitations**: Not a full LMS — no SCORM, no completion certificates, no compliance tracking. But for small teams already on Google Workspace, it's a functional starting point.

## How to Choose the Right LMS

Answer these questions:

**1. Are you training employees or selling courses?**
- Employees only → TalentLMS, Absorb, Docebo
- Selling externally → Teachable, Thinkific

**2. Do you have compliance requirements?**
- Yes (healthcare, finance, manufacturing) → Absorb LMS
- No → TalentLMS

**3. How many users?**
- Under 50 → TalentLMS free or Core plan
- 50-500 → TalentLMS Growth or Docebo
- 500+ → Docebo or Cornerstone

**4. What's your technical capacity?**
- No IT team → TalentLMS (easiest setup)
- Have IT resources → Docebo or Absorb (more configuration options)

## Getting the Most From Your LMS

An LMS is only as good as the training content in it. Common mistakes:

**Don't just upload PDFs**: PDFs are the least engaging training format. Convert to video, interactive quizzes, or scenario-based modules.

**Set completion requirements**: Every course should have a completion percentage threshold (typically 80-100%) before the certificate issues.

**Track and act on data**: Most LMS platforms show course completion rates, quiz scores, and time-to-complete. Use this data to improve weak courses.

**Automate onboarding enrollment**: New hires should be automatically enrolled in your onboarding curriculum the day they start. Don't make this a manual HR process.

The best LMS is the one your employees actually use. Prioritize interface simplicity and mobile access above feature lists.
